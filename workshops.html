Making Crunchy Videos In Handbrake

In October 2022, New Media Club learned how to make "crunchy videos" (a pop culture term for highly compressed, low resolution, low bitrate videos) using the free open-source software Handbrake. We discussed the ways in which "crunchy" videos appear on social media, and how a video/internet artist might use these types of images to represent the passage of time, an expression of low esteem or sarcasm in their work.

Making Art in Kid Pix

In September 2022, New Media Club learned how to use the 1991 version of the popular children's drawing program "Kid Pix", using Archive.org's built in Mac OS 7 emulator. Actions in the program allow you to make abstract artwork very quickly. As someone remarked in club, "I feel like I'm making an NFT!"
